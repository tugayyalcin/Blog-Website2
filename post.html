Marcus Aurelius's Life

Marcus Aurelius was a Roman emperor from 161 to 180 CE. He is considered one of the greatest Roman emperors and is often referred to as the "philosopher-king.

Marcus Aurelius was born in Rome in 121 CE. He was the son of Marcus Annius Verus, a senator, and Domitia Lucilla, the daughter of the emperor Antoninus Pius. Marcus Aurelius received a classical education and studied philosophy under the guidance of several Stoic philosophers.

Marcus Aurelius was also a philosopher and wrote a series of personal reflections known as the Meditations. The Meditations are a collection of Stoic philosophy and are considered one of the most important works of philosophy ever written.

Marcus Aurelius is considered one of the greatest Roman emperors. He was a wise and just ruler who was also a philosopher and a writer. His Meditations are still read today and are considered a valuable source of wisdom and insight.

What is the Stoicisim

Stoicism, the philosophical school that Marcus Aurelius followed, was founded by Zeno of Citium in the 3rd century BC. Stoics believed that the key to a happy and fulfilling life was to live in accordance with nature. They believed that the universe was governed by a rational order, and that humans should strive to live in harmony with this order.

Stoics believed that there were two types of things: things that are within our control, and things that are beyond our control. Things that are within our control include our thoughts, our actions, and our choices. Things that are beyond our control include our circumstances, the actions of others, and the natural world.

Stoics believed that we should focus on the things that are within our control, and that we should not worry about the things that are beyond our control. They believed that we can achieve happiness and fulfillment by living a life of virtue. Virtue, for Stoics, meant living in accordance with nature. It meant being rational, courageous, just, and temperate.

Marcus Aurelius's Meditations are a collection of his personal reflections on Stoicism. In these meditations, Marcus Aurelius discusses the importance of living in accordance with nature, the need to focus on the things that are within our control, and the benefits of living a life of virtue.
